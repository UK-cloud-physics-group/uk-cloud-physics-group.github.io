Presentation guidelines

All participants are expected to present a one-slide lightning talk and an A3 poster on their research. These contributions must be submitted by 11:59 PM Tuesday 17 March. Virtual participants are also encouraged to submit a poster introducing their research.

Lightning Talk

The technical bits: three minutes, one slide, plus one minute for questions. That's four mintues total including the time for questions.

Three minutes? Yes! Three minutes is, intentionally, not enough time to present in-depth results. It is just enough time to introduce yourself: who you are, what drives your work, and what conversations you’d like to have during the event. We want to know who you are as an atmospheric scientist. What are you curious about? What skills do you want to get better at? What’s something you’d like to work on, but haven’t had a chance to so far?

What do I put on the slide? Whatever you feel best introduces yourself and your research interests to the community.

A3 Poster

The technical bits: Posters must be A3 size (landscape preferred). These will be printed and displayed by us. All you need to do is get it to us by 11:59 PM Tuesday.

The purpose of these mini-posters is to provide a visual jumping-off point for conversations about your research interests. Like the lightning talks, the posters are not intended to require a lot of preparation time, or to constitute a professional summary of your work to date. Instead, it's an opportunity to introduce yourself and your research interests. Things you might include: an intriguing result from the literature that is motivating one of your projects; a method or dataset you use or created; a key result from a past or current study. We recommend three figures or less.

Your poster should contain your name, affiliations (institution, any relevant collaborators) and a response to one or more of these questions, or something similar you’d pose to yourself: “I wish I knew somebody else who wants to work on … ” “I think everyone is wrong about … ” “If I never had to think about funding again, I’d research … ” “In my next job/project, I want to…” “I’m thinking about a career-pivot into…” “In my spare time, I … ”

The posters will also be published, with your permission, on the group website to introduce your research and interests to members of the community unable to attend in person. If you do not want your poster displayed online, indicate this in the submission form.
