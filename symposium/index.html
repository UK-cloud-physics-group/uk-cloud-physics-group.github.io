UK Early-Career Cloud Physics Group Symposium

19–20 March 2026

Department of Physics, University of Oxford

About

A two-day research symposium on cloud physics by and for early-career researchers.

This event grew out of a simple observation from our own PhD experience: research groups across the UK don’t often have the opportunity to engage with one another’s work. We thought: why not get everyone together and change that?

The symposium brought together early-career researchers in cloud physics from seven institutions across the UK. All participants presented a lightning talk and poster introducing their research and career to the community. Throughout the two days, we heard from Prof Philip Stier, A/Prof Hannah Christensen and Dr Blaž Gasparini on research and career challenges and opportunities in our field.

Event outcomes

  • The UK Early-Career Cloud Physics Group was formed.
  • A committee was created with representatives from each UK institution in attendance.
  • Participants shared their research through talks and posters.
  • The event set the foundation for future meetings and wider participation.
  • Join the mailing list to stay involved: uk-early-career-cloud-physics@gaggle.email

Event details

The programme ran from midday on Thursday to 1 pm on Friday and included keynote talks, lightning talks, poster sessions, breakout discussions, and a social dinner.

Topics included cloud microphysics, aerosol-cloud interactions, turbulence and dynamics, mesoscale organisation, circulation coupling, cloud-climate feedbacks, observations, modelling, machine learning, deep learning, and research tools development.

In-person attendance was prioritised, with a virtual option for participants to submit an A3 poster introducing their research.
